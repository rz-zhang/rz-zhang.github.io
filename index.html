Biography

I am a second-year graduate student at Georgia Tech, advised by Dr. Chao Zhang. My research interest primarily lies in natural language processing, data mining, and machine learning.

Before that, I obtained my bachelor's degree from Zhejiang University. And I completed my undergraduate thesis as a visiting student researcher under the supervision of Dr. Quan Zhang at Harvard Medical School.

Research

My current research involves developing label-efficient models to learn from weak supervision and leveraging knowledge from text data for better generalization on unseen tasks. Fortunately working with my advisor, we focus on the following research thrusts:
  • Label-efficient learning for text data
  • Knowledge extraction in low-resource settings
Previously I have also worked on health monitoring and medical text mining.
