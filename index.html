Biography

I am a Ph.D. student in the Machine Learning Center at Georgia Tech (ML@GT), advised by Prof. Chao Zhang. I am also fortunate to work with Prof. Le Song. My research interest primarily lies in Machine Learning and Natural Language Processing.

Before that, I obtained my bachelor's degree from Zhejiang University. And I completed my undergraduate thesis as a visiting student researcher under the supervision of Prof. Quan Zhang at Harvard Medical School.

Research

My current research involves developing label-efficient models to learn from weak supervision and leveraging knowledge from text data for better generalization on unseen tasks. Fortunately working with my advisor, we focus on the following research thrusts:
  • Interactive learning for text data
  • Knowledge extraction in low-resource settings
Previously I have also worked on health monitoring and medical text mining.
